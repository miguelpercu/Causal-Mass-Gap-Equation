\begin{abstract}
The Causal Mass Gap equation $E(n) = E_{\text{Planck}} \times \kappa_{\text{crit}}^{1/n}$, derived from the Unified Applicable Time (UAT) and Unified Causal Principle (UCP) framework, connects the Planck scale to lower energy scales through a single constant $\kappa_{\text{crit}} = 10^{-78}$. The integer $n$ determines which physical scale emerges, with zero free parameters beyond the independently calibrated $\kappa_{\text{crit}}$. This work presents the complete set of predictions: $n = 4$ yields the QCD confinement scale at 386 MeV, from which we derive the causal coherence length $\lambdabar_{\text{coh}} \approx 5.7$ fm and the membrane-gluon coupling $\alpha_{\text{mem}} \approx 0.35$, comparable to the strong coupling $\alpha_s$. Extending the sequence, $n = 5$ predicts a new physics scale at 3.07 TeV, accessible at the LHC and future colliders, and $n = 6$ predicts a scale at 1.22 PeV, relevant to neutrino mass generation and ultra-high-energy cosmic rays. We provide detailed derivations of all quantities, their observable consequences, and explicit falsification conditions. The coherence length of 5.7 fm places the causal membrane at the mesoscopic boundary between hadronic and nuclear physics. The coupling $\alpha_{\text{mem}} \approx 0.35$ suggests the membrane interacts with gluons at the same strength as QCD itself. All predictions are derived from independently calibrated constants with no parameter adjustment.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%=====================================================================

The Unified Applicable Time (UAT) and Unified Causal Principle (UCP) framework \cite{uat, ucp} establishes $\kappa_{\text{crit}} = 10^{-78}$ as the fundamental limit on retrocausal influence in spacetime. The Causal Mass Gap equation \cite{causal_pillars} formalizes this connection:

\begin{equation}
    \boxed{E(n) = E_{\text{Planck}} \times (\kappa_{\text{crit}})^{1/n}}
    \label{eq:mass_gap}
\end{equation}

where $n$ is an integer that determines which physical scale emerges. Different values of $n$ correspond to different physical regimes: from dark energy ($n = 1/\alpha \approx 0.64$, where $\alpha = \varphi/2 + 3/4 \approx 1.559$) through QCD confinement ($n = 4$) to the Planck scale itself ($n \to \infty$).

This work presents the complete set of predictions from Eq.~(\ref{eq:mass_gap}) for $n = 4, 5, 6$, including previously undocumented quantities derived from the QCD-scale prediction: the causal coherence length ($\lambdabar_{\text{coh}} \approx 5.7$ fm) and the membrane-gluon coupling ($\alpha_{\text{mem}} \approx 0.35$).

\textbf{Previously documented scales} from the same equation include:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item $n = 1/\alpha \approx 0.64$: $E \approx 3.04 \times 10^{-94}$ eV — the dark energy density, matching the observed cosmological constant to approximately $1\%$ \cite{lambda_resolution}.
    \item $n = 4$: $E \approx 3.86 \times 10^{8}$ eV (386 MeV) — the QCD confinement scale, coinciding with $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$ and the hadron mass spectrum.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{This work} documents two new scales ($n = 5, 6$) and two new derived quantities (coherence length and membrane-gluon coupling) that have not been previously published.

\section{Discussion: The Causal Hierarchy of Scales}
%=====================================================================

\subsection{The Natural Emergence of Hierarchies}

The sequence $n = 4, 5, 6, \dots$ generates a natural hierarchy of energy scales spanning from QCD to the Planck scale:

\begin{equation}
    E(4) \approx 3.86 \times 10^{8} \text{ eV} \;\longrightarrow\; E(5) \approx 3.07 \times 10^{12} \text{ eV} \;\longrightarrow\; E(6) \approx 1.22 \times 10^{15} \text{ eV} \;\longrightarrow\; \cdots \;\longrightarrow\; E(\infty) = E_{\text{Planck}}
\end{equation}

Each increment of $n$ increases the energy scale by a factor of approximately $10^{3.6}$ (from $n=4$ to $n=5$) to $10^{3.0}$ (from $n=5$ to $n=6$). These step factors are not arbitrary — they reflect the distribution of the 78 orders of magnitude of causal suppression across $n$ channels:

\begin{equation}
    \frac{E(n+1)}{E(n)} = (\kappa_{\text{crit}})^{-1/n(n+1)} = 10^{78/n(n+1)}
    \label{eq:step}
\end{equation}

For $n = 4$: $10^{78/20} \approx 10^{3.9} \approx 7.9 \times 10^{3}$. For $n = 5$: $10^{78/30} \approx 10^{2.6} \approx 4.0 \times 10^{2}$. The step factor decreases as $n$ increases, reflecting the convergence toward the Planck scale.

\subsection{The Hierarchy Problem Revisited}

The hierarchy problem in particle physics asks why the electroweak scale ($\sim 100$ GeV) is so much smaller than the Planck scale ($\sim 10^{19}$ GeV). The UAT/UCP framework does not directly address this — the electroweak scale does not appear in the causal sequence $n = 4, 5, 6, \dots$. Instead, the framework generates its own hierarchy: QCD $\to$ TeV $\to$ PeV $\to$ GUT $\to$ Planck. The electroweak scale (Higgs at 125 GeV) sits between $n = 3$ (atomic, 155 eV) and $n = 4$ (QCD, 386 MeV), which is far from any integer $n$.

This suggests that the electroweak scale may not be a ``causal'' scale in the sense of Eq.~(\ref{eq:mass_gap}). It could be generated by a different mechanism —spontaneous symmetry breaking— that operates independently of the causal hierarchy. The causal membrane explains confinement (why quarks are bound) but not mass generation (why particles have mass). The two mechanisms are complementary, operating at different scales and governed by different principles.

\section{Limitations}
%=====================================================================

We explicitly acknowledge the following limitations of the present work:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{The integers $n = 5, 6$ are postulated, not derived from first principles.} While $n = 4$ corresponds to the rank of the 8-phase coherence matrix (the even-parity subsystem is $4 \times 4$), the extension to $n = 5, 6$ requires a formal mapping between the causal membrane and higher-dimensional representations of the Standard Model gauge group. This mapping has not been constructed.
    
    \item \textbf{$\kappa_{\text{crit}} = 10^{-78}$ has an uncertainty of $\pm 0.5$ dex.} This propagates to an uncertainty of approximately a factor of $1.4$ in $E(5)$ and approximately a factor of $1.35$ in $E(6)$. The precise values 3.07 TeV and 1.22 PeV should be understood as central estimates within these uncertainties.
    
    \item \textbf{The coherence length depends on the normalization convention.} Using $\hbar$ vs. $h$, and including or excluding factors of $\varphi$ or $\alpha_{\text{uat}}$, produces values ranging from approximately 0.3 fm to approximately 5.7 fm. The value 5.7 fm is the most physically motivated (using the standard Compton wavelength with the geometric factor), but the ambiguity should be noted.
    
    \item \textbf{The causal membrane is not part of mainstream physics.} Its constants ($\kappa_{\text{crit}}$, $\varphi/2$, $3/4$) have not been independently measured by third-party experiments. The predictions in this work are tests of the UAT/UCP framework, not consequences of established theory.
    
    \item \textbf{Alternative explanations exist.} Even if a signal is detected at 3.07 TeV or 1.22 PeV, it could be explained by physics beyond the Standard Model that is unrelated to the causal membrane. A positive detection would motivate further investigation but would not uniquely confirm the UAT/UCP framework.
    
    \item \textbf{Experimental accessibility varies.} The TeV scale ($n = 5$) is within current collider reach; the PeV scale ($n = 6$) requires next-generation neutrino and cosmic ray facilities that may not reach definitive sensitivity until the 2030s or beyond.
\end{enumerate}

        \item The \textbf{membrane-gluon coupling} $\alpha_{\text{mem}} \approx 0.35$, comparable to the strong coupling $\alpha_s$ at hadronic scales.
